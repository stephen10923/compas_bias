What is this project?

COMPAS (Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions) is an AI tool used by US courts to predict whether a defendant will reoffend within two years. In 2016, ProPublica proved it was racially biased against Black defendants.

This project replicates that investigation end-to-end — loading the real dataset, training models, detecting bias using formal metrics, applying mitigation techniques, and producing a complete audit report.

Core findingBlack defendants receive a False Positive Rate of 35.0% vs 23.5% for white defendants. This means Black people who would NOT reoffend are still labelled "high risk" at 1.5× the rate of white people in the same situation.

1
Data loading & cleaning
01_data_loading.py

Goal: Download the real ProPublica COMPAS dataset and apply the same filters ProPublica used in their 2016 investigation.

  • Downloads dataset from ProPublica's GitHub automatically
  • Keeps only relevant 10 columns (age, race, priors, score, etc.)
  • Applies 4 filters: ±30 day arrest window, valid charge, non-null score
  • Result: 6,172 clean records from the original ~7,214
  • Saves to data/compas_clean.csv

Exploratory data analysis
02_eda.py

Goal: Visualise the racial disparity in COMPAS scores before any modelling — showing the bias exists in the raw data itself.

  • Score distribution histograms: Black defendants skew high (mean 4.8), white defendants skew low (mean 3.4)
  • Actual recidivism rates by race — showing the gap in predictions is NOT justified by actual recidivism
  • High/Medium/Low label breakdown per racial group
  • Box plot of score spread per group

3
Model training
03_model_training.py

Goal: Train ML classifiers that replicate COMPAS's decision-making, then inspect feature importance to identify proxy variables.

  • Trains Logistic Regression (~67% accuracy) and Random Forest (~70% accuracy)
  • 70/30 train-test split, stratified by label
  • 5-fold cross-validation for robustness
  • Feature importance reveals race is the 3rd most important feature (22%) — proxy bias confirmed
  • Saves test predictions with race metadata for Phase 4
data/test_predictions.csv output/charts/feature_importance.png output/metrics/model_performance.csv
4
Bias detection
04_bias_detection.py

Goal: This is the core of the project. Compute per-group confusion matrices and all formal fairness metrics to prove and quantify the bias.

  • Per-group confusion matrices for each racial group separately
  • Computes FPR, FNR, TPR, Accuracy per group
  • Key finding: AA FPR = 35.0% vs Caucasian FPR = 23.5%
  • Formal metrics: Disparate Impact (0.61), Statistical Parity Diff (+0.201), Equal Opportunity Diff (-0.115), Average Odds Diff (+0.183)
  • All 5 metrics confirm bias — none pass the fairness threshold
output/charts/per_group_fpr.png output/charts/confusion_matrices.png output/charts/fairness_metrics.png output/metrics/per_group_metrics.csv output/metrics/fairness_metrics.csv
5
Fairness mitigation
05_mitigation.py

Goal: Apply two fairness mitigation strategies and compare their effectiveness — showing both improvement and the fundamental accuracy vs fairness trade-off.

  • Strategy 1 — Reweighing: Assign higher training weights to underrepresented (race × label) combinations, forcing the model to learn a more balanced decision boundary. Disparate impact improves to 0.87.
  • Strategy 2 — Threshold Adjustment: Use a lower decision threshold (0.40) for Black defendants, reducing their FPR at the cost of slightly lower overall accuracy.
  • Confirms the Impossibility Theorem: fairness improved but accuracy dropped 2-3%
output/charts/mitigation_comparison.png output/metrics/mitigation_results.csv

Audit report generation
06_report_generator.py

Goal: Auto-generate a complete, professional audit report by reading all saved metrics and formatting them into a structured Markdown document.

  • Reads all CSVs from output/metrics/ automatically
  • Generates a full report with tables pre-filled with real numbers
  • Covers: Executive Summary, Background, Dataset, Model Results, Bias Findings, Mitigation, Ethical Recommendations, References
  • Output is a Markdown file that can be converted to PDF or Word
